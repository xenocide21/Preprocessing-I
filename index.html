About Me

I am a student at Lambda School studying Full-Stack Web Development. My goal is to begin a long term career in the tech field using my skills as a web developer and achieve success within my company. With the technology industry ever expanding, now is the best time to join and build a foundation for the rest of my life. In addition to Web Development, I have extensive history in Automotive service, Computer Technology, audio systems, as well as experience in Construction and Oil field Services.


I would one day like to start my own business, after I have learned even more in the Computer Science field of knowledge. Building, programming, and maintaining computer systems has always been a passion of mine, and will continue to be so well into the future.

Experience

  • 10+ years studying computer technology.I started collecting computers and expanding my knowledge and understanding them at the age of 12. Fascinated by the way they worked I spent years learning the terminology and what each component does, how they interact together and over the years have built dozens of computers for personal use. While employed for a company as a refurbish technician, I restored hundreds of computers to factory settings at pre-determined specs.
  • 10+ years doing major vehicle repairs.I have spent the greater portion of the past 12 years learning how to fix and repair any kind of automotive repair, but mostly specialized in Imports with 4 to 6 cylinders and fuel injected. I can rebuild engines or transmissions, do brake service and repair, tune-ups and more. It started as a hobby just working on my own cars, until I decided to go freelance and do repairs for others. Eventually I did work in a shop doing transmission swaps, and smaller jobs like brakes and drivetrain service.
  • 4 years professional car audio installer.When I got my first car, I spent hours researching car audio systems. From that moment on, I began culminating my knowledge of subwoofers, tweeters, and full range speakers. For a long time I only used prebuilt boxes and whatever hardware I could get from walmart, until the day I worked with a friend to build a custom box. After that point, I began doing freelance car audio installation with custom boxes and wiring kits, with the customers chosen hardware. I would do an entire clean install from the components, to the subwoofers and amps, and in-dash kits with aftermarket head units. I really enjoy the look on peoples face when they hear that bass line!
  • 3 years professional PC repair tech.As mentioned above, I worked for a company that recycled and purchased retired and used electronics. As a "Reuse Technician" it was my job to determine if there was any value after restoring to factory operation with minimum specs. If the cantidates were viable, I would clean, install a fresh securely wiped hard drive, and install a licensed copy of Windows to the unit to prepare it for resale on our sales channels.
